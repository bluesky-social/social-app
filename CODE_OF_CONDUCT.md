# K4M2A Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

K4M2A is committed to creating a respectful, thoughtful, and constructive open-source community.

This project exists to support human awakening, inner transformation, philosophical depth, and collective harmony. Our contributor community should reflect those values in how we communicate, disagree, review work, and collaborate.

We welcome contributors from different backgrounds, cultures, disciplines, traditions, and levels of experience.

## Expected Behavior

Contributors are expected to:

- Communicate respectfully and thoughtfully.
- Assume good intent where reasonable.
- Give constructive feedback.
- Stay focused on the work, not personal attacks.
- Be open to learning and correction.
- Respect different philosophical, spiritual, cultural, and technical perspectives.
- Help make the project accessible to new contributors.
- Keep discussions aligned with the mission of K4M2A and CoSeeker.

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behavior is not acceptable:

- Harassment, intimidation, or personal attacks.
- Insults, mockery, or demeaning language.
- Discrimination based on identity, background, belief, nationality, gender, caste, race, religion, disability, age, or other personal characteristics.
- Sexualized language or unwanted attention.
- Trolling, repeated disruption, or bad-faith argument.
- Publishing private information without consent.
- Spam, manipulation, or deceptive participation.
- Attempts to use the project for hate, exploitation, coercion, or harm.

## Disagreement and Debate

K4M2A welcomes serious inquiry, philosophical disagreement, and rigorous discussion.

However, disagreement should be handled with care.

The goal is not to win arguments. The goal is to improve understanding, improve the project, and preserve a space where sincere people can contribute.

Critique ideas clearly. Do not attack people personally.

## Mission Alignment

Contributions should support the project’s purpose:

- Human development
- Inner transformation
- Reflection
- Learning
- Community
- Wisdom
- Safety
- Open-source collaboration

The project should not be used to build addictive engagement systems, manipulative social mechanics, exploitative growth loops, or tools that undermine user dignity and well-being.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may take action when behavior violates this Code of Conduct.

Actions may include:

- A private warning
- A public clarification
- Temporary restriction from participation
- Closing or locking issues or discussions
- Removing comments
- Blocking contributors from the project

Enforcement decisions should be made carefully and proportionately.

## Reporting

If you experience or observe unacceptable behavior, report it to the maintainers.

You may report by opening a private communication channel with a maintainer or by using any official contact method listed by the K4M2A project.

Reports should include:

- What happened
- Where it happened
- Who was involved
- Any relevant links, screenshots, or context

Maintainers should handle reports with discretion and care.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- GitHub issues
- Pull requests
- GitHub Discussions
- Community calls
- Documentation spaces
- Project-related chat or communication channels
- Any public or private space where people represent K4M2A or CoSeeker

## Closing Principle

We are building open-source infrastructure for awakening and human development.

The way we build must be consistent with what we are building.
